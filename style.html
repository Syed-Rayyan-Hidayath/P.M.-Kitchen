Hyderabadi Biryani

The crown jewel of Hyderabadi cuisine, this biryani is made with fragrant basmati rice, marinated meat, and a special blend of spices. Cooked in "dum" style, it results in a flavorful, aromatic dish that’s loved worldwide and embodies Hyderabad’s rich culinary heritage.

Haleem

A famous slow-cooked stew made with wheat, barley, and mutton, Haleem is especially popular during Ramadan. Known for its smooth texture and deeply satisfying flavor, it’s often garnished with fried onions, ghee, and lemon juice, representing comfort food in Hyderabad.

Mirchi ka Salan

A spicy, tangy curry made from green chilies and a rich, nutty gravy of sesame, peanuts, and tamarind, Mirchi ka Salan is typically served alongside biryani. It balances the flavors of biryani with its distinct, complex taste, showcasing Hyderabad’s love for spicy flavors.

Pathar ka Gosht

Marinated mutton cooked on a heated stone slab, Pathar ka Gosht has a smoky, tender flavor. Traditionally cooked over an open flame, it’s loved for its unique texture and taste, making it a popular choice at gatherings and festivals in Hyderabad.

Hyderabadi Marag

A creamy mutton soup with a mild spice blend, Marag is a popular starter at weddings and festive meals. Made with tender meat, it has a rich, comforting flavor and is often enjoyed with naan or as a warm first course in traditional meals.

Baghara Baingan

A tangy, nutty curry made with baby eggplants, Baghara Baingan is cooked in a gravy of peanuts, sesame seeds, and tamarind. Often served as a side with biryani, it’s a must-have for eggplant lovers and highlights Hyderabad’s unique use of spices and ingredients.

Mutton Dalcha

A hearty stew made from lentils and mutton, Mutton Dalcha combines tangy tamarind with warming spices. This dish is traditionally paired with rice and provides a balanced mix of protein and flavor. Its comforting taste makes it a popular choice in Hyderabadi households.

Nihari

A slow-cooked stew traditionally eaten for breakfast, Nihari is made with mutton or beef shank. Cooked overnight, it develops a rich, flavorful broth that’s perfect with naan. Originating from Mughlai cuisine, it’s cherished for its deep, aromatic flavors and tender meat.

Hyderabadi Dum ka Murgh

A traditional chicken dish, Dum ka Murgh is marinated in yogurt, spices, and herbs, then cooked slowly in a sealed pot to retain its moisture and flavor. The result is tender, aromatic chicken with a deliciously creamy gravy, commonly served at weddings and festive gatherings.

This unique biryani is made by layering raw marinated mutton with basmati rice and cooking it "dum-style." The meat tenderizes as it slowly cooks with the spices, creating a rich, flavorful dish with perfectly cooked, fragrant rice. It’s a popular choice among biryani enthusiasts for its distinct flavor.

Talawa Gosht

Fried mutton pieces marinated in spices, Talawa Gosht is known for its crispy exterior and juicy interior. Typically served as an appetizer or side, it’s garnished with lemon, onions, and chilies, adding a burst of freshness to its deep, savory flavor.

Hyderabadi Khichdi

A comforting dish of rice and lentils, Hyderabadi Khichdi is lightly spiced and cooked with ghee for a rich flavor. Often served for breakfast, it’s paired with tangy "khatta" chutney or keema curry. Unlike other khichdis, it has a mild taste, making it a soothing choice for any meal.

Lukhmi

This square-shaped pastry is Hyderabad’s take on samosas, filled with minced mutton and deep-fried to golden perfection. Crispy on the outside with a savory filling, Lukhmi is a popular snack, especially at events, offering a delightful contrast to the city’s more elaborate dishes.

Mirchiyan

A beloved Hyderabadi snack featuring large green chilies stuffed with a flavorful spicy filling, coated in a gram flour batter, and deep-fried to golden perfection. Crispy on the outside, the chilies pack a fiery punch inside. Often served with a sprinkle of chaat masala or tangy tamarind chutney, Mirchiyan is a popular street food that highlights Hyderabad's love for bold and spicy flavors. This delightful snack is especially enjoyed during the monsoon season.

A festive Eid dessert, Sheer Khurma is a creamy vermicelli pudding made with milk, dates, nuts, and saffron. It’s a celebratory dish that’s both sweet and fragrant, representing Hyderabad’s vibrant and diverse food culture, especially during festive gatherings.

Double Ka Meetha

Similar to bread pudding, Double Ka Meetha is made by frying bread slices in ghee, soaking them in sugar syrup, and topping with cardamom, saffron, and nuts. It’s a beloved dessert for its rich, sweet flavor and is often enjoyed during celebrations and special occasions.

Qubani ka Meetha

A traditional dessert made from dried apricots, Qubani ka Meetha is stewed with sugar until it turns into a thick, luscious syrup. Often served with fresh cream or ice cream, this dish is a favorite at weddings and festive events, celebrating Hyderabad’s fondness for fruity desserts.

Khubani ka Murabba

A sweet-tangy preserve made from apricots, Khubani ka Murabba is simmered with sugar and cardamom. This accompaniment adds a refreshing flavor contrast to spicy dishes and is often enjoyed with parathas or plain rice, highlighting Hyderabad’s love for sweet and savory combinations.

Badam ki Jali

A delicate almond-based dessert, Badam ki Jali is crafted from almonds, sugar, and cardamom, shaped into intricate, lace-like patterns. This sweet is not only delicious but also visually captivating, showcasing the craftsmanship of Hyderabadi cuisine. Traditionally made for special occasions, it’s a unique treat from Hyderabad.
