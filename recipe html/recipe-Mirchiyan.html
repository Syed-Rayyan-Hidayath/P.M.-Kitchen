Mirchiyan

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 10-12 large green chilies (mild or medium spicy)
  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for the batter)
  • Water as needed (to make batter)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Wash and pat dry the green chilies. Make a small slit along the side of each chili, being careful not to cut all the way through.
  2. In a mixing bowl, add gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin seeds, salt, asafoetida, and 1 tablespoon of oil. Mix well.
  3. Gradually add water to the mixture, stirring until you achieve a thick, smooth batter.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Dip each chili into the batter, coating it well. Carefully drop it into the hot oil.
  6. Fry the chilies until golden and crispy, turning occasionally for even cooking.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with tamarind chutney or mint chutney.

Extras

Mirchiyan is a popular street snack, often enjoyed during monsoon season. You can stuff the chilies with spiced mashed potatoes or paneer for a more filling version.
