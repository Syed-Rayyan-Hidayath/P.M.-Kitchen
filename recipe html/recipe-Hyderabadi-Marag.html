Hyderabadi Marag

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton with bones (preferably ribs)
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 4-5 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups water (adjust as needed)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and fry for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the mutton pieces and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until they are lightly browned.
  4. Stir in the yogurt, black pepper powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the green chilies, coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Mix everything together.
  6. Pour in water, stir, and bring the mixture to a boil. Once it starts boiling, lower the heat, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 45-60 minutes, until the mutton is tender and the flavors are well blended.
  7. Adjust the consistency of the stew by adding more water if needed. Hyderabadi Marag is typically thin and soup-like.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot. It pairs well with naan or parathas.

Extras

Hyderabadi Marag is traditionally served as a starter at weddings and gatherings. For a richer taste, you can add a touch of cream or cashew paste to the stew.
