Kachche Gosht ki Biryani

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton, cut into medium pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup fried onions
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • A few saffron strands (soaked in warm milk)
  • Whole spices (2-3 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, 2 green cardamoms)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, salt, fried onions, and half of the fresh coriander and mint leaves. Marinate for at least 1-2 hours (preferably overnight for best results).
  2. In a large pot, boil water with whole spices and a pinch of salt. Add soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done. Drain the rice and set aside.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread the marinated mutton evenly at the bottom.
  4. Layer the partially cooked rice on top of the marinated mutton. Sprinkle the remaining coriander and mint leaves, saffron milk, and ghee on top.
  5. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, sealing it with dough if possible, to trap the steam.
  6. Place the pot on low heat and cook for 45-60 minutes, until the mutton is tender and the rice is fully cooked.
  7. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving.
  8. Serve hot with raita or a side of your choice.

Extras

Kachche Gosht ki Biryani is a traditional Hyderabadi dish, known for its layered flavors. For extra richness, you can add fried onions and boiled eggs as garnish.
