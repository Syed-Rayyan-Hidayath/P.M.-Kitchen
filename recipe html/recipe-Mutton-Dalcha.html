Mutton Dalcha

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

For the Mutton

  • 500g mutton, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee

For the Dal

  • 1 cup split yellow pigeon peas (toor dal), rinsed
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups water

Other Ingredients

  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

Cook the Dal

  1. In a pot, combine the rinsed dal, turmeric powder, salt, and water. Cook until the dal is soft and mushy. Mash it slightly and set aside.

Cook the Mutton

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker or large pot. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add mutton pieces, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the mutton is browned and the spices are well combined.
  4. Add yogurt and cook for another 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add water if needed, cover, and cook the mutton until tender (use a pressure cooker for faster cooking).

Combine and Finalize

  1. Once the mutton is cooked, add it to the cooked dal along with cumin seeds, mustard seeds, slit green chilies, garam masala, and coriander powder.
  2. Simmer the mixture on low heat for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  3. Adjust salt and spices to taste. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.

Extras

Mutton Dalcha is a hearty Hyderabadi dish often served with rice or roti. You can add a handful of tamarind pulp for a slight tangy flavor if desired.
