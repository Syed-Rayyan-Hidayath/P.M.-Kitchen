Baghara Khana

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 3 cups water or as needed
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice in water until it runs clear, then soak it for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon stick. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add the drained rice to the pot, stirring gently to coat it with the spices and oil for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add water and salt to taste, bringing it to a gentle boil.
  6. Cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and cook until the rice is tender and water is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).
  7. Fluff the rice with a fork, garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves, and serve hot.

Extras

For extra flavor, use ghee instead of oil and garnish with fried onions. Serve with Baghara Baingan or a spiced curry for a traditional pairing.
