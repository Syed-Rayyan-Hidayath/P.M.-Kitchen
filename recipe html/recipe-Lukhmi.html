Lukhmi

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed

For the Filling

  • 250g minced meat (preferably lamb or chicken)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

Prepare the Dough

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, ghee, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover and set aside for 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling

  1. Heat a pan and add a small amount of oil. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add minced meat, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the meat is fully done and the moisture evaporates.
  4. Stir in chopped coriander leaves and remove from heat. Allow the filling to cool completely.

Assemble and Fry

  1. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a small square or rectangular shape.
  2. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each dough piece. Fold the edges to enclose the filling, pressing down to seal.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the Lukhmi pieces until they are golden and crispy.
  4. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with chutney or ketchup.

Extras

Lukhmi is a traditional Hyderabadi appetizer, often served at weddings. For a vegetarian version, you can replace the meat with spiced mashed potatoes or paneer.
