Mirchi ka Salan

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 8-10 large green chilies (mildly spicy)
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon tamarind paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Wash and pat dry the green chilies. Slit each chili lengthwise, removing seeds if you prefer a milder taste.
  2. Dry roast the peanuts, sesame seeds, and coconut until golden. Grind to a smooth paste with a little water.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and fenugreek seeds, letting them splutter.
  4. Add the green chilies and saut√© until they soften slightly.
  5. Mix in the turmeric, red chili powder, and the prepared paste. Stir well.
  6. Add tamarind paste, salt, and enough water to create a thick gravy. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until flavors meld.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with biryani or naan.

Extras

Mirchi ka Salan is a traditional Hyderabadi dish, often served alongside biryani. Adjust the chili quantity and spiciness to your preference.
