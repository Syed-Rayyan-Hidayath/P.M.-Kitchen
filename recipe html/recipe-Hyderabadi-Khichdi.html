Hyderabadi Khichdi

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal (lentils)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 small onion, finely sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and moong dal under running water until the water runs clear. Soak for 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and sliced onions, and sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add turmeric powder, soaked rice, and moong dal, and stir for a couple of minutes.
  5. Pour in the water and add salt to taste. Stir well and bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Once it begins to boil, lower the heat, cover the pot, and let it cook on a low flame for about 20-25 minutes, until the rice and dal are soft and well-cooked.
  7. Fluff the khichdi with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and fried onions, if desired.
  8. Serve hot with pickle, yogurt, or a side of your choice.

Extras

Hyderabadi Khichdi pairs well with a tangy tomato chutney or papad. For extra flavor, you can add whole spices like cloves and cinnamon while sautéing the onions.
