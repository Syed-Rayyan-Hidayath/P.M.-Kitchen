Nihari

← Back to Menu

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton or beef shank
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup ghee or oil
  • 1 tablespoon wheat flour (for thickening)
  • 6-8 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Nihari Masala

  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon mace powder

Instructions

Prepare Nihari Masala

  1. Dry roast the fennel seeds, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, cloves, and cinnamon stick in a pan over low heat until fragrant. Allow to cool and grind into a fine powder.
  2. Mix the ground spices with turmeric powder, red chili powder, nutmeg powder, and mace powder. Set aside.

Cook the Nihari

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the mutton or beef shank pieces and cook until browned.
  4. Add yogurt, salt, and the prepared Nihari masala. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well absorbed.
  5. Add water, cover the pot, and let it simmer on low heat for 3-4 hours, or until the meat is very tender and the flavors have developed.
  6. In a small bowl, mix wheat flour with water to create a smooth paste. Add this paste to the pot and stir well to thicken the gravy.
  7. Cook for an additional 15-20 minutes on low heat until the Nihari reaches the desired consistency.

Garnish and Serve

  1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, ginger juliennes, and green chilies. Serve hot with naan or parathas.

Extras

Nihari is traditionally slow-cooked overnight to enhance its rich flavors. For a more authentic taste, prepare the Nihari masala fresh and adjust the spices to your preference.
